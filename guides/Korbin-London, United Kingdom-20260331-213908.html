Getting Around

London's public transport system is excellent and LGBTQ+-friendly. The Underground (Tube) is the fastest way to travel, with stations throughout the city. Consider getting an Oyster Card or using contactless payment for convenient travel on buses, trains, and the Tube. Black cabs are iconic but expensive, while ride-share apps like Uber and Bolt offer competitive prices. Walking is often the best way to explore neighborhoods and discover hidden gems.

Sasquatch Tip: Download the Citymapper app for real-time transport information and the best route options. During your April visit, pack an umbrella and waterproof jacket - London spring weather can be unpredictable, but that's part of its charm!

Safety & LGBTQ+ Information

London is one of the world's most LGBTQ+-friendly cities with a vibrant community and excellent legal protections. The Soho district is the heart of LGBTQ+ nightlife, and you'll find inclusive establishments throughout the city. London is generally very safe for tourists, but use common sense in crowded areas and keep valuables secure. The city has excellent healthcare and emergency services.

Experiences

Six Must-Do Activities

📍 Borough Market Food Tour

Explore London's oldest food market with over 1,000 years of history, sampling artisanal cheeses, fresh breads, and specialty coffee. The market's vegetarian vendors offer incredible plant-based options and organic produce perfect for your dietary preferences.

📍 Thames Riverside Walk

Romantic stroll along the Thames from Tower Bridge to Westminster, passing iconic landmarks like Shakespeare's Globe and Tate Modern. The riverside path offers stunning photo opportunities and connects many of London's cultural attractions effortlessly.

📍 West End Theatre Show

Experience world-class theater in London's legendary West End, from long-running classics like The Lion King to cutting-edge new productions. Many shows have LGBTQ+ themes and the theater district's vibrant atmosphere is perfect for a romantic evening out.

📍 British Museum Discovery

Immerse yourself in 6,000 years of human history at one of the world's greatest museums, home to treasures like the Rosetta Stone and Egyptian mummies. The museum's Great Court provides a stunning architectural backdrop for photos, and entry is completely free.

📍 London Eye at Sunset

Rise 135 meters above London for breathtaking 360-degree views of the city skyline, perfectly timed for golden hour photography. The 30-minute rotation provides ample time for romantic moments and capturing the perfect couple's photo with Big Ben and Westminster as your backdrop.

📍 Hyde Park & Kensington Gardens

Escape the city bustle in London's most famous royal park, featuring the Diana Memorial, Serpentine Lake, and Speaker's Corner. April brings beautiful spring blooms perfect for romantic picnics, and the park connects to Kensington Palace and the Albert Memorial for extended exploration.

Luggage Recommendation

For a 7-day London trip, we recommend a medium-sized suitcase (24-26 inches) plus a daypack for exploring. Choose luggage with four spinner wheels for easy navigation through tube stations and cobblestone streets. A waterproof or water-resistant exterior is ideal for London's unpredictable weather. Pack cubes help organize clothes efficiently and make unpacking easier for couples sharing space.

✈️ Carry-On Requirements from Philadelphia (PHL)

Most airlines allow one carry-on bag (22" x 14" x 9") plus one personal item. Liquids must be in containers 3.4oz or smaller in a quart-sized bag. Electronics larger than a phone should be easily accessible for security screening.
