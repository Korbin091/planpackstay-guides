Transportation

London's extensive public transport system includes the Underground (Tube), buses, and Overground trains, making it easy to navigate the city efficiently. Taxis and ride-share apps like Uber are readily available but can be expensive, especially during peak hours. The Oyster Card or contactless payment methods are the most convenient ways to pay for public transport.

🦶 Sasquatch Tip

Download the Citymapper app for real-time transport updates and the fastest routes. Always stand on the right side of Tube escalators, and be prepared for crowded trains during rush hours (7-9 AM and 5-7 PM).

Packing for April in London

April in London brings mild spring weather with occasional showers, so pack layers and waterproof items for comfortable sightseeing.

Clothing

  • Light sweaters/cardigans
  • Long-sleeve shirts
  • Jeans and comfortable pants
  • Light jacket or blazer
  • Warm layers for evenings
  • Smart casual outfits for dining

Shoes

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Waterproof boots or shoes
  • Smart shoes for dining out
  • Extra pairs of socks
  • Foot cream for all the walking
  • Blister prevention patches

Weather Gear

  • Compact umbrella
  • Light waterproof jacket
  • Scarf for chilly evenings
  • Light hat or cap
  • Sunglasses for sunny days
  • Layering pieces

Tech

London's public transport system is extensive and efficient. Purchase an Oyster Card or use contactless payment for tubes, buses, and trains. Walking is often faster for short distances, and the city is very pedestrian-friendly.

Sasquatch Tip: Download the Citymapper app for real-time transport information and the fastest routes between destinations. Off-peak tube travel (after 9:30 AM) is significantly cheaper!
